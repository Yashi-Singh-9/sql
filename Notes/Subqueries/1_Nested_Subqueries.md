# Nested Subqueries

In SQL, a **subquery** is a query nested inside a main query. When a subquery is placed **inside another subquery**, it is referred to as a **nested subquery**.

## Where Nested Subqueries Are Used

Nested subqueries can be used in:
- `SELECT` statements  
- `INSERT` statements  
- `UPDATE` statements  
- `DELETE` statements  
- Inside another subquery  

## Purpose of Nested Subqueries

Nested subqueries are useful for:
- Handling **complex logic** step by step  
- Performing **advanced data filtering and calculations**
- Solving problems that require **multiple levels of query evaluation**

## Important Considerations

- Nested subqueries can become **complex and harder to read**
- Proper formatting and clear logic are important for maintainability

## Summary

Although nested subqueries can quickly grow in complexity, they are a powerful and essential tool for performing advanced and complex database operations in SQL.
